Cali, Colombia

Cali, the 3rd largest city in Colombia, is the capital of the Valle del Cauca department. It is also known as the Salsa Capital of the world and for good reason… the city lives and breathes Salsa! It is also a city of historical landmarks and architecture and a mix of Spanish, Indigenous and African culture. Cali’s temperature remains in the 80s throughout the year, so make sure to pack accordingly.

Entry Requirements

Entry into Colombia

At this time, Colombian migration authorities do not require proof of vaccination or a negative COVID-19 test. But starting November 16th, the Colombian government will require vaccination cards for individuals over the age of 18 to enter face-to-face events and activities in the country, including bars, cinemas, nightclubs, concerts, among others. To enjoy the most of Cali, including our wedding venue, please bring your vaccination card and your face mask.

While we are not requiring people to take COVID-19 test to attend our wedding, we HIGHLY encourage you to get tested. That way we can enjoy the wedding worry-free of any possible transmission.

Before boarding your flight to and from Colombia, you'll be required to fill out a questionnaire from Check-Mig. Please complete this survey no earlier than 48 hours prior to your flight.

Entry into the U.S.

All airline passengers to the United States ages two years and older must provide a negative COVID-19 viral test prior to entering the United States.

  • If you are vaccinated, you must show a proof of vaccination AND take a COVID-19 Viral Test (PCR or Antigen (Rapid) Test) no more than 3 days before the flight’s departure.
  • If you are unvaccinated, you must take a COVID-19 Viral test (PCR or Antigen Test) no more than 1 day before the flight’s departure.
We compiled a list of COVID-19 Testing Locations, for your convenience. Find the one closest to your hotel and make an appointment. For the latest information on entry requirements into the United States, please click here.

Finally, prior to returning to the US, Colombian migration will require you to fill out the Check-Mig questionnaire once again. Please fill out the questionnaire no earlier than 48 hours before your flight.

Local Currency

The currency in Colombia is the Colombian Peso (COP). The exchange rate between USD and COP is about $3,700 COP for every $1.00 USD but fluctuates daily, you can check the last rate here. You can bring cash and use one of the currency exchanges at the airport; simply withdraw from your card at an ATM (though fees do apply); or speak directly to your bank as they may provide the exchange service directly.

Tips

  1. Google Flights is a great website to search for flights. It also has a “Track prices” function that notifies you by email when the flight price changes. This function can only be activated on the computer site.
  2. Although debit and credit cards are generally accepted in commercial establishments, try to have cash as taxis and smaller businesses only accept cash. It is also useful to have lower denomination bills for tips (For example, if someone helps you with your bags).
  3. Restaurants do not usually include the tip in the total bill, they will usually ask if you would like to include it in the check. The tip is usually 10%.
  4. Although there is Uber in Colombia, it is not the most used transport in the city. Authorized taxis are always available. You can request them with the front desk of your hotel or the establishment where you are.
