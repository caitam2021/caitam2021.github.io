Cali, Colombia

Cali, the 3rd largest city in Colombia, is the capital of the Valle del Cauca department. It is also known as the Salsa Capital of the world and for good reason… the city lives and breathes Salsa! It is also a city of historical landmarks and architecture and a mix of Spanish, Indigenous and African culture. Cali’s temperature remains in the 80s throughout the year, so make sure to pack accordingly.

Local Currency

The currency in Colombia is the Colombian Peso (COP). The exchange rate between USD and COP is about $3,700 COP for every $1.00 USD but fluctuates daily, you can check the last rate here. You can bring cash and use one of the currency exchanges at the airport; simply withdraw from your card at an ATM (though fees do apply); or speak directly to your bank as they may provide the exchange service directly.

Tips

  1. Start looking for flights at least 3 months in advance! Google Flights is a great website to search for flights. It also has a “Track prices” function that notifies you by email when the flight price changes. This function can only be activated on the computer site.
  2. Although debit and credit cards are generally accepted in commercial establishments, try to have cash as taxis and smaller businesses only accept cash. It is also useful to have lower denomination bills for tips (For example, if someone helps you with your bags).
  3. Restaurants do not usually include the tip in the total bill, they will usually ask if you would like to include it in the check. The tip is usually 10%.
  4. Although there is Uber in Colombia, it is not the most used transport in the city. Authorized taxis are always available. You can request them with the front desk of your hotel or the establishment where you are.
